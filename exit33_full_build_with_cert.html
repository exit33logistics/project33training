Curriculum — 4 Core Modules

Each module is 30–45 minutes of focused learning with practical activities, examples, and a checkpoint. Below you will find complete lesson text ready to use in your build.

Module 1: Orientation & Tools

Goal: Setup, readiness, dispatcher scope
Length: 30–45 min

Lesson 1 — What a Dispatcher Actually Does

This lesson defines the freight dispatcher role in plain language. A dispatcher connects carriers (truck drivers/operators) to freight opportunities, coordinates paperwork, and supports carriers through the booking and in-transit process.

Scope & limits: Dispatchers may locate loads, prepare and deliver rate confirmations (RCs), request and archive BOLs, and support carrier communications. Dispatchers must not act as brokers (unless properly licensed) or handle freight payments between shippers and carriers.

Activity: Write a 2–3 sentence role summary you can say on a call: "I help owner-operators find loads and manage paperwork so they can run revenue without administrative overhead."

Key takeaway: Dispatchers support carrier logistics — they do not act as brokers or take custody of freight.

Lesson 2 — 60-Second Readiness Check & Tech Stack

Before you take commercial calls, verify basic readiness: reliable internet, phone access, email, and a place to store documents. Typical tech includes a laptop/tablet, smartphone, free spreadsheet CRM, PDF reader, and cloud storage (Google Drive recommended).

Setup exercise: Create a Google Drive folder titled Dispatch Docs — [YourName]. Add subfolders: Carrier Packets, RCs, BOLs, Invoices.

Quick checklist: Email + phone tested; sample PDF upload; blank spreadsheet CRM ready.

Lesson 3 — Time Blocks, Focus & Note-Taking

Reliable dispatchers are predictable. Block focused times to perform outbound searches, carrier outreach, and live-check-ins. Recommended cadence: two 60–90 minute focus blocks per day plus on-call windows for live loads.

Activity: Draft a weekly schedule showing your daily focus blocks and on-call windows.

Checkpoint: Confirm tech setup and schedule in your Drive folder.

Module 2: Compliance & Documents

Goal: Compliance boundaries & core docs
Length: 30–45 min

Lesson 1 — Compliance Basics & Roles

Understand the parties: carrier (owns authority or runs under a carrier), dispatcher (manages logistics), broker (arranges freight between shipper and carrier), shipper (originator of goods). Dispatchers must avoid activities that legally require broker authority, such as negotiating rates with shippers or taking custody of freight.

Red flags: Pretending to be a broker, handling ACH/settlements, or advertising "we pay drivers" without factoring/clear agreements.

Lesson 2 — Documents Walkthrough

Core documents every dispatcher will encounter: Certificate of Insurance (COI), W-9, Rate Confirmation (RC), Rate Confirmation (RC) template, Bill of Lading (BOL), and Carrier Onboarding Packet.

Purpose: COI proves insurance limits; W-9 provides tax info; RC documents agreed rate and load details; BOL is the shipment's receipt of goods.

Activity: Review a sample W-9 and mark the fields you need to collect from a carrier. Place a copy in your Drive Carrier Packets folder.

Lesson 3 — Load Boards & Lanes 101

Load boards are marketplaces for posted loads (DAT, Truckstop, etc.). "Lanes" refer to frequent origin/destination pairs; knowing lanes helps predict rates and turnaround times.

Activity: Pick a lane between two neighboring states (for example: LA → TX) and note typical freight types and expected transit times.

Checkpoint: Match each document (COI, W-9, RC, BOL) with its purpose in a short list and store it in Drive.

Module 3: Carrier Acquisition

Goal: Build pipeline, outreach, book first load
Length: 30–45 min

Lesson 1 — Carrier Pipeline & Simple CRM

Carriers are your core partners. Your CRM can be a simple spreadsheet with columns: Carrier Name, MC#, Equipment, Contact, City/State, Notes, Status, Last Contact.

Activity: Create a CRM with three mock entries (use realistic but fictional names) and save it to Drive.

Lesson 2 — Outreach Scripts (Cold & Warm)

Scripts give consistency. Example cold script: "Hi, my name is [Your Name] with Exit #33 Logistics—do you take external dispatch support? We help owner-operators book reliable lanes and handle load paperwork. Are you open to a quick call about your availability?"

Warm script: "Hey [Name], [referral] suggested I reach out—want to see if we can help smooth your load flow this month."

Activity: Role-play a 2-minute cold call with a peer or record yourself practicing.

Lesson 3 — Booking Flow: Call → RC → BOL

Booking flow is predictable: find a load → confirm carrier availability → send a Rate Confirmation (RC) → after pickup, obtain the Bill of Lading (BOL) → archive all documents.

Sample RC fields: Load ID, Pickup/Delivery, Rate, Accessorials, Contact, Payment terms.

Exercise: Walk through a sample booking with mock data and create the RC in your Drive folder.

Checkpoint: Simulate a booking flow end-to-end and upload RC + BOL mock files.

Module 4: Live Operations

Goal: Run loads, handle exceptions, invoice cleanly
Length: 30–45 min

Lesson 1 — Live Ops: Check-ins & Tracking

Consistent check-ins build trust. Standard check-ins: pre-pickup confirmation, en-route update, ETA update, and delivery confirmation with BOL attached.

Tool tips: Use shared Google Sheets or SMS for quick updates; where possible, capture GPS or tracking numbers to document progress.

Activity: Draft a 3-point daily check-in script and save it in Drive.

Lesson 2 — Exceptions: TONU, Layover, Detention

Definitions and handling: TONU (Truck Ordered Not Used) — when a truck is ordered but not loaded; Layover — unplanned overnight placement at the shipper/receiver; Detention — time spent waiting to load/unload beyond allowed free time.

Examples: TONU: driver shows up and shipper cancels. Detention: receiver delayed and driver waits 5+ hours.

Documentation: Collect timestamps, photos, and any facility paperwork; flag to invoicing with clear notes.

Lesson 3 — Invoicing, Factoring & Archive

Clean invoicing includes: Invoice, Rate Confirmation, BOL, and any detention/TONU paperwork. Factoring companies buy invoices for immediate cash—learn how to present a clean packet for faster funding.

Activity: Assemble a sample invoice packet (Invoice + RC + BOL) and store it in your Drive archive.

Checkpoint: Submit your invoice mockup to your mentor or peer for feedback.

Assessment & Certificate

Each module ends with a short checkpoint quiz and a practical submission (example: upload RC+BOL mock). Successful completions generate a verifiable certificate with a unique reference ID that learners can share with employers or partners.
